Purpose and Scope:

The Act aims to provide a legal framework for the establishment and regulation of cooperative societies operating in more than one state. It covers various types of cooperative societies, including credit societies, housing societies, consumer societies, farmer societies, and more.

Registration and Governance:

To form a multi-state cooperative society, it must be registered under the Act with the Central Registrar of Cooperative Societies. The Act provides guidelines for the formation, membership, and governance structure of these societies. Societies are required to adopt democratic principles, hold regular general body meetings, and maintain proper records and accounts.

Functions and Objectives:

Multi-state cooperative societies are formed to promote economic and social welfare activities for their members. They aim to facilitate mutual cooperation, provide financial assistance, promote thrift and savings, and support community development.

Rights and Liabilities:

Members of multi-state cooperative societies have certain rights, including the right to participate in decision-making, access information, and receive benefits from the society's activities. Members are also liable to contribute to the funds and meet their financial obligations towards the society. Example

The Act establishes authorities such as the Central Registrar of Cooperative Societies and State Registrars to oversee the functioning and compliance of multi-state cooperative societies. These authorities have the power to conduct inquiries, audit the society's accounts, and take necessary actions for the proper functioning of the societies.

Roles and Powers of Authorities:

Multi-state cooperative societies are required to comply with various statutory provisions, such as filing annual returns, conducting audits, and maintaining proper accounting records. Audits ensure transparency and accountability in the financial operations of the society.
